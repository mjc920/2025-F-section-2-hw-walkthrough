How to Survive a Dinosaur Attack

A kid-friendly, jungle-safe guide (playful & imaginative)

Quick steps

If a dinosaur were nearby (pretend-play or story time!), follow these calm, safe steps. These are for fun and imagination — dinosaurs are not real visitors today.

  • Stay calm and find an adult.
    Shouting or running fast can make things scarier. Look for a grown-up and tell them where you are.
  • Move to a safe hiding spot quietly.
    Good hiding spots are behind big rocks, inside a sturdy building, or behind tall trees — somewhere the dinosaur can't see you.
  • Keep very still and be quiet.
    Dinosaurs (in stories) notice movement and noise first. Pretend you're a statue or a sleeping log.
  • Do not try to feed or touch it.
    Even if it looks friendly, keep a safe distance. Offer a smile in your imagination instead!
  • Use distraction only if an adult says it’s okay.
    Sometimes throwing a soft toy far away (while hiding) is a pretend way to distract — but only with permission.
  • If you must move, go slowly and quietly to an exit.
    Walk very slowly, bend low, and keep behind trees or big cover while an adult leads the way.
  • Follow instructions from adults or helpers.
    Listen to adults — they know how to help and keep everyone safe.
  • After it's safe, talk about what happened.
    Share your feelings, ask questions, and maybe draw a picture of the adventure.
